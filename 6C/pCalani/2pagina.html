What may cause it

The exact causes of anorexia are not fully understood, but it is believed to result from a complex interplay of various factors, including biological, psychological, and environmental influences.

  • Body image concerns: Distorted body image and dissatisfaction with one's appearance play a significant role. Society's stereotypes do not help at all.
  • Perfectionism: People with anorexia often have perfectionistic tendencies and set unrealistically high standards for themselves, including in terms of body weight and shape.
  • Low self-esteem: Low self-esteem and a lack of self-worth can contribute to anorexic behaviors as individuals seek a sense of control and achievement through weight loss.
  • Peer pressure: Social and peer influences, such as comments or comparisons with friends, can play a role in the development of disordered eating habits.

What consequences can it bring

Not only does anorexia create short term consequences but long term ones too, affecting both the physical and psychological aspect.

  • Weakness and fatigue: Inadequate nutrition can lead to muscle wasting and weakness, making physical activities difficult.
  • Growth and hormonal disturbances: In adolescents, anorexia can disrupt normal growth and development, including delayed or irregular menstruation and reduced bone growth.
  • Depression and anxiety: Anorexia is often accompanied by depression and anxiety, which can become more severe as the disorder progresses.
  • Obsession with food and body image: Individuals with anorexia often become preoccupied with thoughts about food, weight, and body image, which can interfere with daily life, also leading to social isolation.
  • Mortality: Anorexia nervosa has one of the highest mortality rates of any psychiatric disorder, with complications such as cardiac arrest or organ failure being the leading causes of death.
