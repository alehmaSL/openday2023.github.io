Be a Good Listener:

Encourage open communication and be a good listener. Let them share their feelings and experiences without judgment. Be patient and empathetic.

Offer Support, Not Pressure:

Offer your support and encouragement without trying to force them to change. Understand that they may feel a loss of control, and pressure can be counterproductive.

Respect Boundaries:

While you can offer support, respect their autonomy. They may not always be ready to accept help, and it's essential to respect their decisions.

Be Patient:

Recovery from anorexia is a process that takes time. Understand that relapses can occur, and be patient and supportive throughout the journey.

Encourage Professional Help:

Suggest seeking help from a qualified healthcare professional, such as a therapist, counselor, or medical doctor who specializes in eating disorders. Offer to assist in finding treatment options and making appointments.
